The Benchmark Problem

⚠️ Lab Benchmarks: Approach with Suspicion

Every AI lab publishes benchmarks showing their model is "best"

  • Cherry-picked metrics: Highlight where they excel
  • Custom evaluations: Designed to favor their approach
  • Timing games: Comparing against outdated models
  • Marketing first: Science second
The Golden Rule

Never trust a benchmark published by the company selling the model

What We Need Instead

  • Independent evaluations: Third-party testing
  • Open methodologies: Reproducible results
  • Task-specific benchmarks: Relevant to your work
  • Real-world performance: Not just test scores

The LLM Jury Approach

Why Use Multiple Models?

Even the best models have blind spots and biases

🎯 Consensus Building

Method: Run same task on 2-3 different models

Benefit: Catches individual model errors

Example: Claude + GPT-4o + Gemini for classification

🔄 Cross-Validation

Method: Models critique each other's outputs

Benefit: Identifies weak reasoning, hallucinations

Example: Model A analyzes, Model B fact-checks

⚖️ Deliberation

Method: Models discuss disagreements

Benefit: Higher quality final decisions

Example: "GPT-4o says X, Claude says Y. Discuss."

📊 Voting Systems

Method: Majority rule or weighted consensus

Benefit: Democratic decision making

Example: 3/5 models agree → high confidence

Cost Warning: Multiple models = multiple API calls. Budget accordingly.

Conclusions & Strategic Recommendations

Core Insights
  • Models are improving exponentially - what's impossible today is routine tomorrow
  • Flaws exist but are addressable - verification protocols work
  • The bottleneck is shifting - from "can we get answers?" to "are we asking the right questions?"

🛡️ Mitigation Strategies

  • LLM Jury: Multiple model consensus
  • Citation Verification: Check sources systematically
  • Staged Verification: Progressive confidence building
  • Human Oversight: Expert review at key points

📈 Leverage Strategies

  • Choose the right model: Task-specific selection
  • Use independent benchmarks: Ignore marketing claims
  • Build verification pipelines: Trust but verify
  • Stay current: Landscape changes monthly
